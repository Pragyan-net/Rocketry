1. Tsiolkovsky Rocket Equation

The fundamental formula for ideal rocket motion is:
Δv = Isp × g₀ × ln(m₀ / m₁)
Where:

  • Isp = Specific impulse (s)
  • g₀ = 9.81 m/s² (Earth gravity)
  • m₀ = Initial total mass
  • m₁ = Final mass after fuel burn

2. Gravity Turn

A technique used to pitch the rocket gradually during ascent. This allows it to use gravity to curve its path efficiently and reach orbit with minimum fuel. We simulate this using angle and velocity vectors.

3. Atmospheric Drag

Drag force is modeled with:
Fdrag = ½ × ρ × v² × Cd × A
ρ is the air density (which decreases with altitude), Cd is drag coefficient, A is cross-sectional area.
We approximate air density using:
ρ = ρ0 × e−h / H where H ≈ 8500 m

4. Staging

Multi-stage rockets drop empty fuel tanks to reduce mass and increase efficiency. You can simulate basic staging by increasing the number of stages — a full implementation would model stage mass, ISP, and burn time separately.

5. Delta-V Budget

Delta-V (Δv) is the total change in velocity needed to reach orbit. For Low Earth Orbit, ~9400 m/s is typically required.
Our simulator helps estimate how close your configuration gets based on thrust, ISP, mass, and burn time.
